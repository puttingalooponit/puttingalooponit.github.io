GDP around the World & The Impact of Covid-19

Dealing with Covid-19 has proven to be a unique financial challenge globally, and so in this webpage, I attempt to paint a picture of the pre-pandemic global economies and also show the impact of Covid-19 on their growth.

Nominal Growth in relation to Economy and Population Size

This scatterplot represents growth relative to Economy size, and using colour differentiates between countries whose economic growth matches or exceeds their population growth. Having a negative population growth, negative economic growth, or slow economic growth in relation to population size are key indicators for a weakening economy.

The Richest Citizens in the World

This visualisation represents the Nominal GDP by Volume per Capita of all the countries & Regions.

Comparing the Financial Crises: Covid-19 vs The Great Recession

In this visualisation, we can see that among the most documented and developed economies of the world, the economic loss brought by Covid-19 is magnitudes greater then that of the The Great Recession. A frightening factor to consider is that with countries jumping in and out of lockdowns, the worst may be yet to come.

This visualisation has been created by Salmaan Khan. The data sources are The World Bank, and the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development.
